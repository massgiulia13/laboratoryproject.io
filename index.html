Cultural object: Fede Sarda

The "Fede Sarda" is a typical ring made of Sardinian filigree used since ancient times by the women of the region. The ring has a high symbolic and cultural value: it was normally handed down from mother to daughter. Today, there are still around several ancient examples of this type of ring. There are many models of this ring, which differ from province to province. The best known are the ones composed of small silver or gold honeycomb filigree curls and those composed of small filigree palmettes that give a leafy effect. The models are made of microspheres. In the Sardinian ring, the filigree dots have not only an ornamental but also a symbolic function, in fact they represented the grains of wheat, a symbol of abundance and prosperity. Documentation

The object chosen is a silver engagement ring called "Fede Sarda". The choice was driven by the symbolic and cultural meaning of this object, linked to the Sardinian tradition.

IIIF Image

IIIF stands for "Image Interoperability Framework". The specifications are developed to help institutions like libraries, archives and museums to make the digitized images in their collections available in a common and interoperable network. The images are enriched with annotations and deep zooms. IIIF standards follow W3C web protocols, use RESTful API construction, JSON-LD and linked data. IIIF gives to scholars, rsearchers and general users to easily share and parse the digitized material. The highest-quality picture taken with LG-G7 smartphone was uploaded on a IIIF image-hosting website. In this way, the creation of the JSON Manifest description of the image, was made possibile.

3D Modeling

In order to obtain a faithful reproduction of the ring, I used the photogrammetry software Zephyr3D. The photos were taken with the integrated camera of the LG-G7 ThinQ smartphone model. The pictures reproducing the ring were 27 (in total), and were combined together to form the sparse and dense point cloud, and the textured mesh. The mesh was then exported via Sketchfab and in .obj/mtl format.
